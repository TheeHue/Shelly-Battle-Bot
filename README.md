# Shelly: Arduino Battle Bot for the UIC Competition

## Overview
Shelly is a battle bot created for the UIC Battle Bots competition. Our team designed the robot with a hexagon-shaped body so it could better withstand hits from enemy bots while still being able to return damage with its front weapon. We built this project to compete against other student teams and to gain hands-on experience with robotics, programming, electronics, and mechanical knowledge.

## Project purpose
The purpose of this project was to design and build a functional robot from scratch. We wanted to create a bot that could survive lots of attacks, move quickly in the arena, and destroy other bots with its weapon. 

## Features / functionality
Shelly was designed with a hexagonnshaped frame for protection during fights. The armor was made with thin titanium plates that faced downward to help defend against hits from other robots. The bot used two tracks on the bottom, which allowed it to move smoothly and complete 360-degree turn. It also included a small saw mounted on the front that was meant to chip away at the armor of our enemies. 

## Tech
The battle bot was programmed using Arduino C/C++. The Arduino code controlled the motor, track movement, turning, and the front-facing weapon. The robot also used a custom controller, which sent input signals that the bot could read and use for movement and knowing what to do with the weapon. For design , we used CAD software to model the robot before building it. Other important components included wiring, electronic parts, tracks, titanium armor plates, and welding equipment.

## Timeline
We started the project by brainstorming ideas on paper and talking about possible designs in small teams. Once we agreed on a general design, we created a model in CAD software so we could better understand the layout of the robot. After that, we planned the wiring and electronics and ordered the materials and parts we needed. When the parts arrived, we spent several weeks assembling the bot, testing it, fixing problems, and reworking certain parts. Once the robot was complete, we entered the battle phase of the competition and competed against others.

## Challenges
One of the biggest challenges in this project was coordinating with teammates and finding times when everyone could meet consistently. Another challenge was the learning curve, since we had very little experience building robots before starting this project. We had to spend a lot of time researching and learning new skills, including wiring, electronics, and welding. Putting all of the parts together into one working robot took a lot of troubleshooting, and testing.
